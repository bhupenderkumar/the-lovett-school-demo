Our Mission & History

The Lovett School was founded in 1926 by Mary Holland Lovett, with a mission to provide a nurturing and academically rigorous education to students in Atlanta. Today, we continue to uphold this legacy, serving students from diverse backgrounds and abilities. Our commitment to community and character is at the heart of everything we do.

At The Lovett School, we believe that learning should be a joyful and holistic experience. Our faculty are passionate about teaching and learning, and we strive to create a curriculum that is both challenging and engaging. From STEM to the arts, we offer a wide range of programs and activities that help our students develop their passions and interests.

The Lovett School is more than just a school - it's a community. Our students, faculty, and families come together to support one another, whether through extracurricular activities, community service, or simply being there for each other. We believe that this sense of belonging is essential to a happy and healthy childhood.
